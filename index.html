About Me

A little about me and my background, and my journey in software development.

What I'm All About

Hi, I'm Ahmed Ikram – a Computer Science student at the University of Dundee with a passion for building things that actually work and solve real problems. I'm currently pursuing my BSc (Hons) in Computer Science with a focus on Data Science and AI, and I'm on the lookout for a 12-month industrial placement where I can dive deep into challenging projects and continue growing as a developer.

I've always been drawn to the intersection of data and development. There's something incredibly satisfying about taking a messy dataset or a vague idea and transforming it into a functioning system that people can use. Whether it's building a full-stack mobile app, engineering a machine learning pipeline, or optimizing backend systems, I genuinely enjoy the entire process – from the initial problem-solving phase right through to deployment.

My technical toolkit is pretty diverse. I'm comfortable working with Python, Java, C/C++, JavaScript, and TypeScript, and I've built projects across the full stack using everything from React Native and Flask to SQL databases and cloud infrastructure on AWS. I'm particularly interested in how machine learning and AI can be applied to real-world challenges, which is why I've focused on data-driven projects throughout my studies.

My Journey So Far

My education at the University of Dundee has given me solid foundations in Data Structures & Algorithms, Multi-Paradigm Programming, and Data Engineering. I've also completed AWS Academy certifications in Cloud Foundations and Machine Learning, which have given me hands-on experience with cloud-native architectures and tools like SageMaker, EC2, and S3.

But honestly, some of my best learning has come from just building stuff. I led an end-to-end machine learning project where I took raw data all the way through exploratory analysis, feature engineering, and model deployment – achieving around 90% classification accuracy with XGBoost and Random Forest. I also built StockLens, a React Native mobile app that uses OCR to scan receipts and turn them into structured digital records. These projects taught me that getting something to work in a Jupyter notebook is one thing, but building a reliable, user-friendly system is a whole different challenge – and that's the challenge I love.

What Sets Me Apart

I think what makes me a bit different is that I don't just focus on the code. Yes, I care deeply about writing clean, efficient, scalable solutions, but I also think a lot about why we're building something and who it's for. My work experience in customer-facing roles – from training staff at Zulus Peri Peri to troubleshooting problems at Beauly Mini Market – has taught me the importance of communication, process optimization, and understanding user needs. These aren't traditionally "tech" skills, but they've been invaluable when it comes to gathering requirements, explaining complex concepts to non-technical stakeholders, and building systems that people actually want to use.

I'm also comfortable learning quickly. When I encounter a new technology, framework, or massive codebase I've never seen before, I don't get intimidated – I get curious. I ask questions, I experiment, I break things (in safe environments!), and I figure it out. That adaptability has served me well in university projects and I know it'll be essential in an industrial placement.

What I'm Looking For

Right now, I'm searching for a placement opportunity where I can contribute meaningfully while continuing to learn from experienced developers and engineers. I want to work on projects that matter – whether that's building scalable backend systems, developing intelligent applications, or working with large datasets to extract actionable insights. I'm excited about roles that will challenge me, push me out of my comfort zone, and help me develop into the kind of developer who can handle complex, real-world problems.

Beyond the Code

When I'm not coding or studying, I'm probably exploring new technologies, contributing to open-source projects, or working on side projects that catch my interest. I believe in continuous learning – not because I have to, but because I genuinely enjoy it. The tech world moves fast, and I like keeping pace.

If you're looking for someone who's technically capable, eager to learn, and genuinely passionate about building great software, let's connect. I'd love to hear about what you're working on and explore how I might contribute to your team.
